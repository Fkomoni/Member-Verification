High Cholesterol: The Silent Risk You Can Control

Hypercholesterolaemia — high levels of cholesterol in the blood — typically has no symptoms at all, yet it is a leading driver of heart attacks and strokes. The encouraging news is that diet and lifestyle changes alone can produce dramatic, measurable improvements.

Understanding Your Condition

Your doctor measures Total Cholesterol, LDL (bad cholesterol), HDL (good cholesterol), and Triglycerides. Aim for LDL below 3.0 mmol/L and HDL above 1.0 mmol/L. If you have been prescribed statins (e.g. atorvastatin, rosuvastatin), do not stop them without consulting your doctor — they work continuously in the background even when you feel fine.

Lifestyle Tips
🫀
Exercise consistently.Aerobic exercise — brisk walking, swimming, cycling — raises good HDL cholesterol and lowers LDL. Aim for at least 150 minutes per week.
🚭
Stop smoking.Smoking lowers HDL and damages blood vessel walls, making cholesterol plaques far more dangerous and unstable.
⚖️
Lose excess weight.Even a 5 to 10% reduction in body weight significantly improves cholesterol profiles across all markers.
🍺
Limit alcohol strictly.Alcohol raises triglycerides and adds empty calories that promote weight gain. Avoid it where possible.
🛒
Read food labels.Avoid products listing 'partially hydrogenated oil' or 'trans fat'. These are the most harmful fats for cholesterol levels.
🧑‍⚕️
Review medications with your doctor.Some medications can raise cholesterol. Ask your doctor to review your full prescription list at each visit.
What to Eat More Of
🐟 Mackerel & tilapia (grilled) 🥑 Avocado pear 🫘 Beans & brown beans 🌾 Oats & whole grains 🥜 Groundnuts (unsalted) 🥬 Ugu, okra & green soups 🫒 Olive oil for cooking
Limit or Avoid
Fried foods & excess suya Organ meats (shaki, excess ponmo) Palm oil in large quantities Pastries & butter Full-fat dairy in excess Trans fat products
⚠️ High cholesterol rarely acts alone. It commonly coexists with hypertension and diabetes. If you have any two of these three conditions together, your heart and stroke risk rises sharply — make sure all of them are being actively monitored and treated.
