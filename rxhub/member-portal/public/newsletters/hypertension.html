Living Well with High Blood Pressure: Your Everyday Guide

High blood pressure (hypertension) is one of the most common chronic conditions among Nigerians — yet with the right daily habits it can be managed very effectively. Here is what every member living with hypertension should know.

Understanding Your Condition

Hypertension means your blood pressure is consistently at or above 140/90 mmHg. Often called the 'silent killer', it rarely causes symptoms yet quietly damages the heart, kidneys, and blood vessels over time. The key message is simple: you can live a long, healthy life with hypertension if you manage it consistently.

Lifestyle Tips
🚶
Walk daily.A brisk 30-minute walk five days a week can lower blood pressure by 5 to 8 mmHg on its own.
🧂
Reduce your salt intake.Limit added salt in cooking and reduce seasoning cubes (Maggi, Knorr) — they are extremely high in sodium.
⚖️
Maintain a healthy weight.Losing even 5 kg can produce a meaningful reduction in blood pressure readings.
🚭
Quit smoking and limit alcohol.Both directly raise blood pressure and increase your risk of stroke and heart failure.
😴
Sleep 7 to 8 hours nightly.Poor or broken sleep keeps blood pressure elevated — consistency matters as much as duration.
🧘
Manage stress actively.Daily prayer, deep breathing, or a short walk after work all help lower the stress hormones that spike your pressure.
What to Eat More Of
🍌 Bananas 🥬 Waterleaf & vegetables 🐟 Grilled fish 🥣 Oatmeal 🥑 Avocado pear 🫘 Beans & lentils 🥛 Low-fat dairy
Limit or Avoid
Excess table salt Seasoning cubes (excess) Fried & smoked foods Soft drinks & juice drinks Processed canned foods Excess palm oil
⚠️ Never stop your medication because you feel fine. Take it at the same time every day, even when your readings look normal. Missing doses is one of the leading causes of stroke in Nigeria.
