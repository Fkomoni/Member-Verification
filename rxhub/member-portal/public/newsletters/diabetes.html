Managing Diabetes in Nigeria: Small Changes, Big Results

Type 2 diabetes is increasingly common in Nigeria, particularly in urban areas. It does not have to control your life. With the right knowledge and day-to-day habits, you can keep your blood sugar stable and continue living fully.

Understanding Your Condition

Your target fasting blood sugar is generally between 4.0 and 7.0 mmol/L (72 to 126 mg/dL). Your HbA1c — a 3-month average — should ideally be below 7%. Monitor regularly and bring your log to every doctor's appointment. Consistency in medication, diet, and movement are the three pillars of good diabetic control.

Lifestyle Tips
🕖
Eat at regular times.Skipping meals causes blood sugar swings. Three measured meals is better than one large meal, regardless of how busy you are.
🏃
Exercise regularly.Physical activity helps your body use insulin better. Even a 20-minute walk after dinner makes a real difference.
👣
Inspect your feet every evening.Diabetes reduces sensation in the feet. Check for sores, cuts, or swelling daily — small wounds can become serious quickly.
💊
Take your medication as prescribed.Whether insulin or oral tablets, consistency matters more than anything else. Never self-adjust doses.
💧
Stay well hydrated.Drink water consistently throughout the day. Avoid malta, fruit juice drinks, and Ribena — they spike blood sugar rapidly.
🩺
Annual eye and kidney checks.Diabetes silently affects both organs over time. Do not skip your annual screenings even when you feel perfectly well.
What to Eat More Of
🥬 Bitter leaf soup 🫘 Moi moi (less oil) 🐟 Grilled or baked fish 🥣 Unripe plantain 🥜 Garden egg 🌾 Ofada rice (small portions) 🍵 Unsweetened zobo
Limit or Avoid
White rice (large portions) Eba & fufu (large portions) Sugary drinks & alcohol Bread & pastries Ripe plantain & ripe banana Malta & fruit juice drinks
⚠️ Low blood sugar emergency: If you feel dizzy, shaky, or excessively sweaty, eat a small amount of sugar or drink fruit juice immediately, then contact your doctor. Inform a family member about this protocol so they can help you.
