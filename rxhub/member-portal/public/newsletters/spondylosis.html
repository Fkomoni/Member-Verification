Spondylosis and Back Pain: Managing Your Spine Every Day

Spondylosis refers to the natural wear and tear of the spine — affecting the neck (cervical), mid-back (thoracic), or lower back (lumbar). It is extremely common in adults over 40 and, with the right approach, is very manageable without limiting your daily activities.

Understanding Your Condition

As we age, the discs between vertebrae gradually lose their cushioning ability. This can cause stiffness, aching, or nerve pain that radiates to the arms or legs. Poor posture, prolonged sitting, and heavy lifting all accelerate symptoms. The core goal is to reduce pressure on the spine and progressively strengthen the muscles that support it.

Lifestyle Tips
🪑
Correct your sitting posture.Sit upright with both feet flat on the floor. If you work at a desk, your screen should be at eye level — not tilted down.
🧘
Stretch every morning.Gentle spinal stretches on waking ease morning stiffness significantly. Ask your physiotherapist to design a 5-minute safe routine.
🏊
Prioritise low-impact exercise.Swimming and walking are excellent for spondylosis — they strengthen the spine without compressing the affected discs.
🛏️
Sleep on a medium-firm mattress.Very soft mattresses allow the spine to curve unnaturally overnight and worsen symptoms by morning.
📱
Reduce text neck.Avoid looking down at your phone for long periods. Hold it at eye level. Text neck accelerates cervical spondylosis.
🏋️
Lift correctly, always.Bend your knees, not your back, when lifting anything from the ground. Avoid carrying heavy bags on one shoulder only.
What to Eat More Of
🐟 Oily fish (omega-3) 🫚 Ginger & turmeric 🥬 Green leafy vegetables 🍊 Citrus fruits (Vitamin C) 🥛 Low-fat dairy (calcium) 🌰 Sesame seeds 💧 Water (for disc hydration)
Limit or Avoid
Processed & packaged foods Excess red meat Alcohol Sugary drinks Deep fried foods
⚠️ See your doctor promptly if you notice: numbness or weakness in the arms or legs, loss of bladder or bowel control, sudden severe worsening of pain, or pain that consistently wakes you from sleep. These may indicate a more serious spinal complication that needs urgent attention.
