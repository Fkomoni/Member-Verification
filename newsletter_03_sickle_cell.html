Thriving with Sickle Cell: A Guide for Members and Caregivers

Nigeria carries the highest burden of sickle cell disease in the world. Living with SCD requires specific daily care, but with the right approach — and the right support — many people with sickle cell live active, productive, and fulfilling lives.

Understanding Your Condition

A sickle cell crisis (severe pain episode) occurs when misshapen red blood cells block blood flow in small vessels. Common triggers include dehydration, cold temperatures, infections, overexertion, emotional stress, and exposure to the harmattan. Identifying your personal triggers is your single most powerful prevention tool.

Lifestyle Tips
💧
Hydrate aggressively.Drink at least 8 to 10 glasses of water daily. Dehydration is the most common and preventable crisis trigger in Nigeria's heat.
🌡️
Avoid temperature extremes.Carry a light jacket in air-conditioned environments. Avoid cold water baths and prolonged harmattan exposure.
💊
Take folic acid every day.Folic acid supports red blood cell production. Do not stop taking it, even on days when you feel completely well.
🦟
Prevent malaria strictly.Malaria is a major sickle cell crisis trigger. Sleep under insecticide-treated nets and take malaria prophylaxis as advised by your doctor.
😴
Rest before you are exhausted.Overexertion triggers crises. Learn to pace yourself and take breaks — rest is medical management, not weakness.
🏥
Know your nearest emergency centre.Before a crisis happens, identify your closest SCD-competent hospital. Keep your blood type card and doctor's number on your phone.
What to Eat More Of
🥩 Fish, eggs & beans (protein) 🍊 Vitamin C rich fruits 🥬 Dark leafy vegetables 🌰 Groundnuts & seeds 🌿 Ginger & turmeric 💧 Water — always
Limit or Avoid
Alcohol Excessive caffeine Cold water or iced drinks Smoking Iron supplements (unless prescribed)
⚠️ Seek emergency care immediately for: severe chest pain, difficulty breathing, stroke symptoms (face drooping, arm weakness, slurred speech), very high fever, or priapism. These are life-threatening emergencies — do not wait and manage at home.
